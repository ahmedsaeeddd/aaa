About Me

Football & Player

The player Mohamed Salah was born in the village of Najrig, in Gharbia Governorate, on June 15, 1992. Since his childhood, Mohamed Salah was passionate about football, as it was his top priority during his growing up years. Like any child who loves football, Mohamed Salah used to watch his favorite players play. On television with great interest.

Salah began his first steps in his professional career with the “Arab Contractors” Junior Club in Cairo, where he used to travel from his hometown of Nagrig to Cairo every day. He would take five buses to reach his club and participate in training. Salah remained dedicated, diligent, and immersed in his training, until he reached the age of 18 and then joined the first team of the Mokawloon Club. Mohamed Salah showed a great level with his team in the Egyptian Premier League, and reserved a permanent place for himself in the team’s starting lineup. Some political circumstances in the country led to the postponement of all Egyptian Premier League matches, and Egyptian football players faced a crisis regarding the fate of their careers, but fortunately for Salah, his wonderful display of skills in the matches he played with his team was eye-catching for the management of one of the most important clubs in the Swiss League. "FC Basel". Salah's name became popular in the Champions League after his wonderful performance, as Chelsea then concluded a deal with Basel to include him in the club's ranks, and indeed Mohamed Salah joined the club on January 26, 2014, becoming the first Egyptian football player to sign for Chelsea. A few days later, he made his debut as a substitute for his team in the English Premier League against Newcastle United. Salah continued to score goals regularly, match after match, and by the end of the season, Salah moved to Italian club Roma after an 18-month loan period. Mohamed Salah appeared for the first time with Roma in 2015, and showed an outstanding performance in this season until he deserved the Player of the Season award with distinction. Salah was the top scorer in this tournament with 15 goals. Mohamed Salah's exceptional skills caught the attention of English Premier League coaches once again, when Liverpool offered Roma a huge sum of 50 million euros to buy Mohamed Salah, which was considered a record in the history of the club's deals. Therefore, Salah agreed and signed a long-term contract to move to Liverpool in June. 2017 to be the first Egyptian football player to move to this club.
